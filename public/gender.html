Comparison of Gross Domestic Product and Per Capita

It may happen to all of us eventually, but fewer of us are dying right now than at any time since mortality data was collected. In a dramatic report the latest figures also show that more people are dying of cancer than of circulatory diseases, for the first time since the data was recorded. The latest figures from the Office for National Statistics show there were 484,367 deaths registered in England and Wales in 2011, a fall of 1.8% compared with 2010. The age-standardised mortality rates (which takes account of aging population) in 2011 were the lowest ever recorded for England and Wales, at 6,236 deaths per million population for males and 4,458 deaths per million population for female.
